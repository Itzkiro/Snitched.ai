# Florida GOP Gubernatorial Primary 2026 — Candidate Positions & Red Flags

Research compiled 2026-04-22 for Snitched.ai. Focus areas: Israel/AIPAC posture, America First, scandals, donors, and endorsements. DeSantis is term-limited; primary is August 18, 2026.

Polling snapshot (April 2026): Donalds ~82% on Polymarket; Emerson poll ~46% Donalds, Collins ~15%, Fishback single digits.

---

## 1. BYRON DONALDS (R-FL-19, U.S. House)

Frontrunner. Trump-endorsed February 2025. Announced $22M Q1 2026 fundraising; ~$67M cycle total — record non-incumbent pace.

### A. Israel / Gaza / Iran / Palestine

- Declared "We should stand behind Israel 100%." On Oct 7 anniversary: "Donalds Stands With Israel And Jewish Americans" (official House press release).
- Voted YES on HR 8369 (Israel Security Assistance Support Act, 2024) preventing withholding of weapons to Israel.
- Cosponsored HR 1102 (UNRWA Accountability and Transparency Act).
- Cosponsored H.Res. 771 (Standing with Israel against Hamas).
- Voted YES on H.Res. 845 (Censuring Rep. Rashida Tlaib).
- Took an AIEF-sponsored trip to Israel in May 2019 (while in FL House, pre-Congress).
- Keynote speaker at Republican Jewish Coalition (RJC) summit; called by RJC leadership "a good, close friend and ally."
- Predicted shift in Israeli strategy during 2026 Iran escalation; supportive of Israeli strikes on Iran.
- NO public statements conditioning aid to Israel, calling for AIPAC FARA registration, or endorsing two-state solution. Consistently "Israel First" per Track AIPAC's labeling.

### B. AIPAC & pro-Israel money

- Per Track AIPAC: **~$84,000+** from AIPAC and the Israel lobby as of mid-2024 (likely higher now).
- OpenSecrets: 2020 cycle ~$6,000 pro-Israel PAC + $2,778 individuals; 2022 ~$14,400 PAC + $20,127 individuals (trajectory increasing).
- Track AIPAC explicitly labels him "Israel First."

### C. America First posture

- House Freedom Caucus member; high Heritage Action score.
- Trump-endorsed; frequent Trump surrogate; was on VP shortlist in 2024.
- Hawkish-interventionist on Israel/Iran; no public stance for cutting Israel aid.
- Anti-DEI, school-choice, hardline immigration.

### D. Scandals / criminal history / ethics

- **1997 marijuana distribution arrest** (age 18) — pre-trial diversion, fined $150; Donalds has admitted he was "dealing."
- **2000 felony theft/fraud charge** (bad-check bank scheme) — pled no contest; record later sealed and expunged. (NOTE: caller's brief cited 2007/2013 dates; actual cases are 1997 and 2000.)
- **Wife Erika Donalds charter-school conflict (ACTIVE, the biggest vulnerability):**
  - Erika runs Optima Foundation + for-profit OptimaEd and Optima Management Solutions.
  - Florida Bulldog (2025-2026 investigative series) documented $15M+ in taxpayer-funded contracts flowing from charter schools (Treasure Coast Classical, Naples Classical, Jacksonville Classical) into her nonprofit, with significant sums switched over to her for-profits ($5.8M+ in one year).
  - January 2026 lawsuit alleges her charter business cut side deals with a construction firm.
  - Byron Donalds **failed to disclose his wife's stake** in Optima Management Services to House Ethics; he amended 2023 filing and first-ever 2024 filing only AFTER Florida Bulldog exposé.
  - Long-running financial entanglement with FL State Rep. John Snyder, who paid Erika Donalds salary through a payroll-services firm tied to Optima academies.
  - GOP primary opponents (Dec 2025) already attacking over "abandoned" school and "possibly fraudulent" H-5 visas.
- Contentious Naples town hall (spring 2025) over DEI and Gaza questions.
- Top advisor has "disturbing arrest history" (Florida Trident reporting).

### E. Donors & endorsements

- Trump (Feb 2025). 9 FL Republican state senators endorsed April 2026.
- Naples-area real estate, financial services, insurance donors (his former Wells Fargo/State Farm background).
- Wife's charter-school network.
- No public RJC PAC endorsement for governor yet as of April 2026 (RJC's 2026 midterm endorsements announced March 2026 covered congressional, not gubernatorial).

## 2. JAMES FISHBACK (Azoria Partners founder, 30)

Hedge-fund outsider. Launched campaign November 2025. Rapidly became the race's wild card via an unusual MAGA-populist anti-AIPAC posture.

### A. Israel / Gaza / Iran / Palestine (the standout)

- **Most anti-AIPAC GOP gubernatorial candidate in memory.** Tweeted: "I'll be the first to admit that I fell for the 'Israel is our greatest ally' scam and the lie that criticizing Israel is 'antisemitic'… It wasn't until I was offered a paid trip to Israel this summer (which I never took) that I realized how cringe and pathetic the propaganda."
- Called Netanyahu "an immoral war criminal."
- Pledges to direct all Florida government entities to **divest from Israel Bonds** and reinvest into state housing assistance.
- Explicitly calls **AIPAC "a foreign lobbying group"** and pledges to refuse any donations from organizations affiliated with foreign countries. Effectively a FARA-adjacent argument — exceedingly rare on GOP side.
- April 2026: Issued "antisemitic ultimatum" (per VIN News framing) to Palm Beach County official over Israel Bonds investment.
- Cited Holy Family Church bombing + "Gaza genocide" language as triggers for his position change.
- Pledges to revoke Florida's pro-Israel censorship laws.
- Uses "goyslop" framing in online content.

### B. America First posture

- Hard anti-legal-immigration ("America Is Full" essay in The American Conservative).
- Designed the Trump/Musk-endorsed **"DOGE Dividend"** ($5,000-check proposal from federal savings).
- NOT Trump-endorsed for governor.
- Rejected by Republican Jewish Coalition as "a radical fringe candidate who has decided that the way to run his campaign is to attack the Jewish community and our ally Israel."

### C. Scandals / legal exposure (MASSIVE)

- **Greenlight Capital litigation:** Fishback's ex-employer David Einhorn's firm sued him over (a) false representation that he had been "head of macro" when he was a research analyst; (b) diverting work-time to his debate nonprofit; (c) attempting to defraud Greenlight's match program by claiming donations he hadn't made and producing "false evidence"; (d) sharing confidential Greenlight positions via personal email and running an undisclosed trading account.
- **January 2026 federal magistrate order:** Fishback ordered to surrender Azoria stock and luxury items to US Marshals for sale to satisfy $229K judgment to Greenlight.
- Ordered separately to repay $337K+ in defaulted loans.
- April 2026: Narrowly avoided contempt ruling.
- **Azoria Meritocracy ETF (anti-DEI S&P 500)** shut down October 2025 by the fund's own independent trustees over legal concerns. Fishback claims to have left Azoria January 2026.
- **Incubate Debate:** Broward County Schools cut ties in 2022 over allegations he groomed an underage girl during in-person tournaments.
- Court filings show Fishback **used fake social-media accounts and paid engagement** to shape his press coverage.
- Substack/independent reporting ("Scammer, Liar, Grifter") also references car repossession.

### D. Donors & industry ties

- Self-funding ~$5M+ but Q4 2025 / Q1 2026 fundraising weak — only $19K reported by mid-January 2026 outside self-funding.
- Hedge-fund alumni network (Greenlight, Bridgewater); relationship with Einhorn now adversarial.
- Bill Ackman publicly supportive of some Fishback efforts (DOGE-adjacent), though not a formal campaign endorsement.
- Anti-DEI investing thesis donors (Azoria).

### E. Endorsements

- None of note. RJC publicly hostile. No Trump, no Freedom Caucus PAC, no Club for Growth, no AIPAC PAC.

## 3. JAY COLLINS (FL Lieutenant Governor; former State Senator SD-14)

Appointed LG by DeSantis August 12, 2025. Announced gubernatorial bid January 12, 2026 — running against Trump's endorsed candidate.

### A. Israel / Gaza / Iran / Palestine

- Traveled to Israel as FL State Senator in 2023/2025 to help evacuate trapped Americans after Hamas attacks.
- Publicly praised Trump's Israel-Hamas peace deal: "Kudos to President Trump… that's what leadership looks like."
- Told Newsmax to "show compassion to released hostages" (October 2025).
- Solidly pro-Israel aligned, though record is state-legislative rather than federal (no AIEF trip documented via AIPAC; his Israel travel appears self/state-organized).
- No record of opposing unconditional Israel aid, calling for AIPAC FARA registration, or supporting two-state conditioning.
- Had at least one documented meeting with Fishback (June 2025, pre-Fishback announcement) that Florida Politics framed as friendly; Collins has NOT endorsed Fishback's anti-AIPAC line.
- Called Donalds (April 2026): "not a risk that we can afford to take."

### B. America First posture

- Pro-Trump but DeSantis-loyal; fits DeSantis-brand Florida-first conservatism.
- NOT Trump-endorsed (Trump endorsed Donalds).
- Veteran/national-security profile implies hawkish posture; no record of dovish foreign-aid positions.
- In State Senate: hardline immigration, anti-DEI, gun rights, veterans.

### C. Scandals / red flags

- **Remarkably clean record.** 20+ years Army Special Forces, Bronze Star, Purple Heart, Legion of Merit, Soldier's Medal. 2007 Afghanistan: shot in arm, performed self-surgery mid-firefight. Lost leg years later from those injuries; re-qualified as fully deployable Green Beret afterwards.
- Main political vulnerability: appointed LG by a lame-duck DeSantis and running against Trump's pick — the "DeSantis loyalist" frame is the attack vector (Trump-world has signaled displeasure).
- No documented ethics complaints, no criminal history, no business-conflict reporting surfaced.
- State Senate voting record is standard Florida GOP (supported DeSantis priorities on immigration enforcement, parental rights, election integrity). Detailed 2024 Iran-bill-specific FL Senate record not surfaced; state-level Iran votes are rare.

### D. Donors & industry ties

- Tampa Bay business community (SD-14 = Hillsborough). Veteran-community donor base. Home Base / veterans' advocacy ties.
- No hedge-fund or charter-school baggage.
- Likely DeSantis-network big donors (Tampa Bay, central FL real estate).

### E. Endorsements

- Backed implicitly by DeSantis-world (appointment itself is endorsement).
- No Trump, no RJC, no AIPAC PAC endorsement surfaced.
- Veteran/military organizations favorable.

## Cross-candidate notes

- None of the three have publicly called for AIPAC FARA registration EXCEPT Fishback, who functionally calls AIPAC a foreign-lobbying group.
- None have endorsed two-state solution or conditioning aid EXCEPT Fishback (who goes further — full divestment).
- Only Donalds has a documented AIEF-sponsored Israel trip.
- Donalds = pro-Israel hawk + ethics baggage (wife's charter-school conflicts, past criminal record); Fishback = rare anti-AIPAC GOP voice but legally/ethically compromised; Collins = clean but Trump-disfavored.
