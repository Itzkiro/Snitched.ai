# TN Governor 2026 GOP Primary — Opposition Research: Blackburn & Fritts

**Race:** Tennessee Governor 2026 (open seat — Gov. Bill Lee term-limited).
**Primary date:** Aug 6, 2026. **General:** Nov 3, 2026.
**Field (GOP):** U.S. Sen. Marsha Blackburn (frontrunner ~56–58%), U.S. Rep. John Rose, State Rep. Monty Fritts, plus ~dozens of minor filers.

Sources per candidate: Ballotpedia, Wikipedia, Tennessee Lookout, NewsChannel 5, OpenSecrets, TrackAIPAC, candidate sites, Senator.gov official releases.

## 1. Sen. Marsha Blackburn (R) — Establishment frontrunner

### Bio snapshot
- b. 1952 (age 74). U.S. Senator TN 2019–present; U.S. Rep TN-07 2003–2019; TN State Sen. 1999–2003.
- Announced gubernatorial bid Aug 6, 2025. Does not have to give up Senate seat (reelected Nov 2024 to a 6-year term ending Jan 2031).
- Q1 2026 haul: $1.23M. Cumulative since launch: ~$6.73M.
- Polling March 2026 (Cygnal): Blackburn 58 / Rose 7 / Fritts 4.

### A. Israel / Gaza / Iran / Palestine positions

**Among the Senate's most hawkish pro-Israel voices.** Maintains a standalone "Israel" issues page on blackburn.senate.gov enumerating her record.

Key actions:
- **Military aid:** "The United States must provide Israel with every available resource — including sending any and all military assistance — to defend itself with overwhelming force against terrorists." Voted in 2025 to continue arming Israel; co-sponsored May 2024 resolution condemning any Biden withholding of weapons/ammunition for Israel.
- **Cosponsor, U.S.-Israel Security Assistance Authorization Act (2018)** — codified full funding under 2016 MOU.
- **Taylor Force Act** — supported cutoff of U.S. funds to PA while it pays "martyr" stipends. Later co-sponsored the Taylor Force Martyr Payment Prevention Act (2023).
- **UNRWA:** Introduced legislation to halt all U.S. funding to UNRWA; voted to cut UNRWA funding.
- **Gaza refugees:** Joined 34 senators opposing admission of Gazans to the U.S.
- **Iran:** Introduced Iran Nuclear Deal Advice and Consent Act (2021) requiring Senate ratification. Led Senate Republicans demanding permanent freeze of Biden's $6B Iran payment, characterizing it as "ransom." Opposed the original JCPOA while in the House.
- **Jerusalem embassy:** Supported the move.
- **Anti-BDS:** Cosponsored Israel Anti-Boycott Act in the House.
- **Other:** Introduced Hizballah in Latin America Accountability Act (2021); U.S.–Israel AI R&D Center Act (2021); No Immigration Benefits for Hamas Terrorists Act (2024); co-sponsored placing pro-Hamas protesters on FAA no-fly lists (May 2024).

**AIPAC FARA registration call:** No. She is a recipient of AIPAC/UDP/NorPAC support, not a critic.

### B. Israel-lobby group ties
- **AIPAC / pro-Israel lobby total:** TrackAIPAC/"Bought by Zionism" page reports **$587,466** cumulative pro-Israel-lobby money to Blackburn. A separate TrackAIPAC tweet cited $328,000+ from AIPAC specifically as of 2024. She is consistently ranked among AIPAC's top-receiving senators.
- **CUFI:** Confirmed panelist on CUFI Virtual Summit 2020 "View from the Hill" alongside Cruz, Rubio, Cotton, Lankford, Scott, Rosen. Her legislation has been actively endorsed by CUFI, RJC, and ZOA per her Senate press releases.
- **RJC:** No direct 2026 gov endorsement confirmed as of April 2026, but RJC has repeatedly praised her Israel record; she has been a regular at RJC events.
- **AIEF trip:** Blackburn has spoken at AIPAC Policy Conference multiple times; details of any AIEF-funded Israel delegations are in her historical House travel disclosures (pre-2019).

### B.2 America-First posture & foreign aid generally
**Not an America-Firster in the Tucker Carlson/MTG mold.** She supports foreign aid with friction only on adversaries: opposes Iran aid, UNRWA, Palestinian Authority, but supports Israel aid and Ukraine aid historically (her Ukraine posture softened post-2023 to align with Trump). Consistently hawkish on China (TikTok, trade, Uyghur sanctions). Hard-line restrictionist on immigration ("planes, trains or starships" deportation line at launch).

### C. Scandals / investigations / career controversies
- **CREW "Most Corrupt" list, 2010:** Named by Citizens for Responsibility & Ethics in Washington over alleged campaign-finance improprieties.
- **Daughter / son-in-law firm funneling:** Congressional Integrity Project alleged ~$370,000 funneled to "basement-run firms" owned by family members.
- **DEA / opioid bill (Ensuring Patient Access and Effective Drug Enforcement Act, 2016):** Blackburn was a House lead. DEA whistleblower Joe Rannazzisi publicly alleged her staffers were warned the bill would hamstring DEA's ability to freeze suspicious opioid shipments; 60 Minutes/WaPo exposé in 2017 forced then-AG-nominee Tom Marino to withdraw. Major reputational hit; not prosecuted.
- **Planned Parenthood Select Panel (2015–2016):** Chaired the committee; referrals and statements widely fact-checked as misleading (e.g., FactCheck.org on "94% of PP revenue is abortion" claim).
- **January 6, 2021:** Announced intent to object to certification on Jan 2; after the Capitol was breached, voted **to certify** the election. Still named in House Jan 6 Committee testimony — Sen. Hagerty told investigators his vote was "in sync" with Blackburn's.
- **Stock trades:** No documented personal COVID-era insider-trading scandal (unlike Loeffler/Perdue). In fact she co-sponsored a bipartisan bill to ban Member/spouse stock trading.
- **Abortion votes:** Consistent anti-abortion record including federal 15-week ban cosponsor; repeated Planned Parenthood defunding votes.
- **Big Tech / KOSA:** Lead Senate sponsor with Blumenthal of the Kids Online Safety Act. Tech-policy press (Techdirt Dec 2025) criticized her Dec 2025 omnibus bundle slapping Trump's name on it, describing it as an attempt to revive previously-stalled speech-regulation bills.

### D. Donors and industry ties
Per OpenSecrets career data on Sen. Blackburn (CID N00003105):
- Historically top industries: **pharmaceuticals/health products, telecom/AT&T (TN-home-state), tobacco, finance/securities, defense, big tech**.
- 2023 (pre-gov-race) Q1–Q3: raised $7M, with $5.1M from PACs.
- Personal wealth modest per WKMS/Tennessee Lookout FEC analysis (March 2026): she built the gov campaign without self-funding, relying on transferred leadership PAC cash and bundled donors.

### E. Endorsements as of April 22, 2026
- **Trump:** No endorsement yet in the primary (Trump publicly neutral between Blackburn and Rose).
- **Club for Growth PAC:** Endorsed Blackburn (cites her Senate tax/regulatory record).
- **Americans for Prosperity (AFP) State PAC:** Endorsed Blackburn (Feb 26, 2026).
- **Heritage Action:** No formal 2026 endorsement to date; she scores highly on their scorecard historically.
- **RJC / AIPAC-PAC / NorPAC / UDP:** No confirmed 2026 gov primary endorsement; all have backed her federally.
- **JStreet / dovish groups:** None — hostile.

### Snitched signal summary — Blackburn
- **Israel-aid stance:** Maximalist. Unconditional military aid, no-ceiling, anti-conditions.
- **AIPAC ties:** Deep. ~$587K lifetime pro-Israel lobby $; CUFI speaker; anti-BDS sponsor.
- **Foreign-aid posture:** Pro-Israel/Ukraine/anti-Iran/anti-UNRWA; conventional GOP hawk, NOT America-First isolationist.
- **Biggest red flags:**
  1. DEA opioid-bill sponsor — tied to the crisis-fueling legislation that sank Marino AG nomination.
  2. CREW "Most Corrupt" 2010 + Congressional Integrity Project allegations of family-firm funneling.
  3. Record-holder for pro-Israel lobby $ among TN delegation; clear vector for "Israel First" Snitched tag.

## 2. State Rep. Monty Fritts (R) — Anti-establishment grassroots challenger

### Bio snapshot
- b. Dec 10, 1963 (age 62). Rockwood, TN (Roane County). Army veteran. Retired nuclear-industry worker (local Oak Ridge corridor — K-25 / ORNL / Y-12 adjacent).
- Education: Liberty University B.S. Business & Chemistry, M.A. Theological Studies; University of Tennessee MBA.
- TN House District 32 (Roane County + parts of adjacent) since Jan 2023. Succeeded Kent Calfee.
- Announced governor bid Sep 8, 2025.
- Campaign sites: **fritts4tn.com** (gov) and **fritts4tn32.com** (house).
- Poll standing: ~4% Cygnal March 2026 / ~11% TNSUCCESS March 2026.

### A. Israel / Gaza / Iran / Palestine positions

**Much thinner public record than Blackburn** — state rep with no federal floor votes. Best documentary source is his **iVoterGuide** survey response (candidate #66849).

- **BDS (Boycott-Divestment-Sanctions):** On iVoterGuide, Fritts answered **"Strongly Disagree"** to the prompt "I support the Boycott, Divestment, Sanctions (BDS) movement..." → **He is anti-BDS**, i.e. pro-Israel in the Christian-Zionist framing. (An earlier summary suggested he supported BDS; direct inspection of the iVoterGuide page shows the opposite.)
- **Foreign aid generally:** iVoterGuide — "all foreign aid should be stopped until a balanced budget is achieved." This is a blanket statement with no Israel-exemption carve-out, putting him in tension with Blackburn and with the typical CUFI-aligned "aid to Israel is different" framing.
- **Intervention:** "The U.S. should not intervene in other sovereign nations' political affairs." Non-interventionist lean.
- **Iran:** No direct public statement located; his "strong national defense + diplomacy" language on Islamic terrorism does not specify Iran policy.
- **Gaza / IDF / hostages:** No located public statement.
- **AIPAC FARA registration call:** No documented call for it, but his overall grassroots anti-establishment posture and "stop all foreign aid" line make him the nearest thing to an AIPAC skeptic in the TN gov race. He is the opposite pole from Blackburn on foreign-aid mechanics even though he is not explicitly anti-Israel.

Net read: **Christian-Zionist-adjacent (anti-BDS, Christian-Nationalist self-label) but foreign-aid-restrictionist** — an internally tense combination, but a clear America-First "stop writing checks" posture that makes him genuinely different from Blackburn.

### B. Christian Nationalist / religious-right posture
- NewsChannel 5 Investigates (March 2026): Fritts, asked about the "Christian Nationalist" label, said "if someone puts that label on me, I think that's not necessarily a negative thing." He self-identifies as a Christian and America First.
- **CUFI / RJC:** No known CUFI speakerships, no RJC ties.
- Authored HJR 51 (2025) encouraging prayer and intermittent fasting in July.
- "Godly values" and "creationist worldview" rhetoric central to campaign.

### B.2 America First / foreign aid / trade / immigration
- **Foreign aid:** Stop all foreign aid until U.S. budget balanced. No Israel exception stated.
- **Immigration:** Has written the country should "suspend legal immigration until we can restore our cultural values" — positions him to Blackburn's right on immigration.
- **Trade/China:** Not a developed public record at state level.
- **Trump alignment:** Runs as "America First" — but Trump has not endorsed.

### C. Scandals / investigations / controversies
- **Execution rhetoric (Jan 2026) — national scandal:** Leaked podcast clip (later amplified by Right Wing Watch) in which Fritts said that parents, guardians, and medical professionals connected to gender-affirming care for minors "might be eligible to capital punishment" — quote: "I think that anyone who would try to disfigure a child through hormones or surgery, you might be eligible to capital punishment." Widespread condemnation in national LGBTQ press (PinkNews, Instinct, Hollywood411).
- **Earlier leaked abortion clip:** Prior leaked audio in which he advocated capital punishment for people who have undergone or facilitated abortions. The Jan 2026 trans-parent clip was a continuation of that pattern.
- **Geoengineering / "chemtrails" bill (SB 2691 / HB 2063, 2024):** Lead House sponsor of a bill banning intentional atmospheric chemical release "for the express purpose of affecting temperature, weather, or the intensity of the sunlight." Covered by NBC News, Tennessee Lookout, PolitiFact, AVweb as a chemtrails-conspiracy-influenced bill; Fritts denied chemtrails framing but critics called it "nonsense."
- **Opposed voucher expansion:** One of very few Rs to vote against Gov. Lee's private-school-voucher program — cost him with establishment GOP and Heritage-aligned groups.
- **Cousin-marriage-ban vote (April 11, 2024):** One of only **two** House members to vote **against** SB 1917 / HB 2041 banning marriage between biological first cousins. Highly-circulated opposition point.
- **Armed teachers vote (April 5, 2023):** Voted to advance bill arming teachers, weeks after the Covenant School shooting in Nashville.
- No located ethics complaints or criminal record.
- **FARA concerns:** None documented.

### D. Donors and industry ties
- Nuclear-industry retiree, Roane County — directly adjacent to **Y-12 National Security Complex** (Anderson Co.), **Oak Ridge National Laboratory** (split Roane/Anderson), and the former **K-25** site (Roane Co.). Has been a vocal supporter of Oak Ridge-corridor nuclear expansion (Oklo advanced-fuel center, Orano uranium enrichment).
- As a state rep in a cheap media market, his FEC/FREPC donor profile is modest; full donor list for his gov campaign was not publicly itemized as of the April 2026 research cutoff. Recommend checking TN Registry of Election Finance filings and TNSOS campaign-finance portal for Q1 2026 disclosures.
- No known corporate-PAC pipeline comparable to Blackburn's pharma/telecom/AT&T base.

### E. Endorsements as of April 22, 2026
- **Trump:** No. (Neutral in primary.)
- **Club for Growth PAC:** No — Blackburn has it.
- **Heritage Action:** No.
- **House Freedom Caucus-affiliated PACs:** None located.
- **RJC / AIPAC-PAC / NorPAC / UDP / JStreet:** None located.
- **Friendly-press validation:** Fringe-right outlets (e.g., "Liberty, Leadership and Lies with Larry Linton") have framed him as "the only true conservative choice against establishment pretenders."
- As of April 2026, Fritts is effectively unendorsed by any major state or national conservative PAC/interest group.

### Snitched signal summary — Fritts
- **Israel-aid stance:** Not explicitly pro- or anti-Israel-aid; supports cutting ALL foreign aid until budget balanced → functionally anti-Israel-aid in effect, anti-BDS on principle.
- **AIPAC ties:** None documented. No AIPAC, CUFI, RJC, NorPAC, UDP ties located.
- **Foreign-aid posture:** Blanket "stop all foreign aid" — closest thing in the race to a genuine America-First isolationist on the dollars question.
- **Biggest red flags:**
  1. Calls for **capital punishment** for parents of trans minors and for women who have abortions — national firestorm Jan 2026.
  2. "Christian Nationalist" self-acceptance on-camera.
  3. Chemtrails-adjacent geoengineering bill; anti-cousin-marriage-ban vote — signals conspiracy and fringe positioning.

## 3. U.S. Rep. John W. Rose (R) — Wealthy Cookeville businessman challenger

### Bio snapshot
- b. Feb 23, 1965 (age 61). Cookeville, TN (Putnam County). Farmer, IT-entrepreneur.
- Education: B.S. agribusiness economics Tennessee Tech (1988); M.S. agricultural economics Purdue (1990); J.D. Vanderbilt Law (1993).
- U.S. Rep TN-06 since Jan 2019 (now in 4th term, 119th Congress). Previously TN 33rd Commissioner of Agriculture (2002–2003) under Gov. Sundquist.
- Business: Co-founded **Transcender Corp.** (1992), IT-certification training; sold Oct 2000 for $60M. Currently owner/president of **Boson Software, LLC** (IT training, Cookeville). Also owns Rose Farm (Tennessee Cattlemen's Assn past president).
- **Personal wealth: ~$48M–$56M range** per various public estimates — among the wealthiest members of the TN delegation, wealthier than both Blackburn and Fritts. Disclosed >$600K in stocks plus full ownership of Rose Farm (≥$5M).
- Family: Wife Chelsea Doss Rose (Miss Tennessee 2002); two sons (Guy, Sam). Controversy: met Chelsea when she was a 17-year-old HS senior (he was judging a state student contest in 2007); married 2011 when she was 21 and he was 45. Rose has defended the gap publicly (compared to Trump/Melania).
- Announced gov bid **Mar 20, 2025** — first in the field. Q1 2026 total receipts leading: ~$6.4M+ (per his camp).
- Poll standing: ~7% Cygnal March 2026 (Blackburn 58 / Rose 7 / Fritts 4).

### A. Israel / Gaza / Iran / Palestine positions

**Fully hawkish pro-Israel voice — indistinguishable from Blackburn on substance.** Has repeatedly taken the House floor in support of Israel.

Key actions and statements:
- **H.R. 815 / supplemental structure (April 2024):** When H.R. 815 was restructured into separate vehicles in the House, Rose voted **YES on H.R. 8034 (Israel Security Supplemental, $26.38B)** and **NO on H.R. 8035 (Ukraine Security Supplemental, $60.84B)**. His press release: "Our only Democratic ally in the Middle East, Israel, is in dire need of immediate assistance… I have always stood in steadfast support of Israel and will continue to do so until they emerge victorious in their battle against Hamas." Also voted against the Ukraine funding "without a realistic strategic plan from the Biden Administration."
- Rose has supported foreign aid for Israel on **at least three separate 2024 votes** per his own press operation.
- **House floor speeches:** Multiple in 2023-2024 explicitly declaring "unwavering support for Israel" — including in the hours before PM Netanyahu's July 2024 joint address to Congress.
- **Iran:** Calls Iran "one of the greatest forces of evil in the world" and calls Hamas "a proxy of Iran." Supported Trump administration strikes on Iranian nuclear facilities; believes U.S. must "stand ready to protect Israel" so nuclear capabilities are reduced in the region.
- **Palestinian Authority:** Cosponsor of the Palestinian International Terrorism Support Prevention Act.
- **Gaza / IDF / hostages:** Full support for IDF operations; frames conflict as Israel's fight "until they emerge victorious." No public call for ceasefire conditions or humanitarian carveouts located.
- **AIPAC FARA registration call:** None. Rose is squarely in the CUFI/Christian-Zionist congressional bloc.

### B. Israel-lobby group ties
- **TrackAIPAC:** Rose does **not** appear on TrackAIPAC's "Bought by Zionism" spotlight page for Tennessee (Blackburn is the focus there). He is less visible as an AIPAC direct-donation target than Blackburn — consistent with his House (not Senate) status and his comparatively self-funded profile — but his voting record tracks AIPAC's priorities 100%.
- **CUFI:** No confirmed CUFI Summit speakership located. His public Israel rhetoric is fully CUFI-aligned in style.
- **RJC:** No confirmed 2026 gov endorsement from RJC. His record is RJC-friendly but he has not been a headline RJC speaker.
- **NorPAC / UDP / AIEF:** No trip disclosures or bundling visible in public reporting as of April 2026 cutoff. (Recommend pulling his House travel disclosures 2019–2024 for any AIEF-funded Israel delegation.)

### B.2 America First posture & foreign aid generally
**"Israel-exception America First"** — the standard GOP hawk pattern. Supports cutting foreign aid in general ("put America first," "take care of our own") **except** Israel, which he treats as categorically different. Voted NO on Ukraine supplemental; YES on Israel supplemental. On immigration he is a hard-line restrictionist, calling the border "a humanitarian and national security crisis" and demanding end to catch-and-release. No developed public China/trade record beyond generic GOP talking points. Heritage Action scorecard: **100% (119th), 100% (117th), 98% (116th)** — deep movement-conservative.

### C. Scandals / investigations / controversies
- **January 6 / 2020 election denial:** Signed amicus brief supporting Texas v. Pennsylvania (the Paxton lawsuit to throw out 2020 results in swing states). Voted to **object to certification** of the 2020 electoral count on Jan 6, 2021. Republican Accountability Project gave him an **F rating**.
- **Capitol Police Gold Medal vote (June 2021):** Among the small minority of House Republicans who voted **AGAINST** awarding the Congressional Gold Medal to Capitol Police who responded to Jan 6. Rose said "it was too soon."
- **Disaster relief block (May 2019):** Single-handedly blocked a $19.1B bipartisan disaster relief bill in the House on deficit grounds, drawing rare national rebuke from his own party.
- **Fiscal Responsibility Act 2023:** Voted NO with the 71-member GOP rebellion against the Biden-McCarthy debt ceiling deal.
- **Chelsea Doss Rose age-gap marriage controversy:** The American Prospect (April 2022) published a detailed piece on the "ethical concerns" over Rose meeting his wife when she was a 17-year-old HS senior at a state student contest he was judging (2007), their 24-year age gap, and the financial/educational power imbalance before their 2011 marriage (he 45, she 21). Rose has publicly defended it. Not criminal; a reputational/character vector.
- **Viral son C-SPAN moment (June 2024):** Rose's 6-year-old son Guy made faces on the House floor during a speech on Trump's criminal conviction. Went viral nationally. **Biographical only — not a red flag.**
- **Ethics complaints / FARA:** None located in federal records as of April 2026.
- **Stock trades:** No documented insider-trading scandal. Per disclosures, >$600K in stocks — but no whistleblower-level allegations.

### D. Donors and industry ties (qualitative)
- Financial-services committee member (Subcommittees: Digital Assets, FinTech, AI; Financial Institutions; Housing & Insurance). Also House Agriculture Committee (Vice-Chair, Commodity Markets/Digital Assets/Rural Development subcommittee).
- Base donor profile tilts toward **Tennessee agribusiness/cattle** (he is a past Tennessee Cattlemen's Assn president), **crypto/digital assets** (via his Financial Services role), **community banks** (Financial Institutions subcommittee), and **IT training/edtech** (his Boson Software sector).
- Because of his personal wealth (~$48M+), he is less dependent on PAC money than Blackburn — his gov campaign's lead fundraising ($6.4M in first year) is fueled partly by in-state small-donor/grassroots reporting plus a self-funding capability.
- **Defense industry exposure:** Modest; not one of his top-industry clusters per OpenSecrets member profile (CID N00041599).
- Cookeville/Putnam County is his geographic base; has visited all 95 TN counties during gov campaign.

### E. Endorsements as of April 22, 2026
- **Trump:** **No 2026 gov primary endorsement** as of April 22, 2026. Trump has praised Rose publicly ("he was fantastic," "endorsed me in mine") and previously endorsed Rose's congressional runs, but Trump has not endorsed either Rose or Blackburn in the 2026 gov primary — he is publicly neutral.
- **Club for Growth PAC:** **Blackburn has it**, not Rose.
- **Americans for Prosperity (AFP) State PAC:** **Blackburn (Feb 2026)**, not Rose.
- **Heritage Action:** No formal 2026 gov endorsement; Rose scores **100%** on their scorecard.
- **RJC / AIPAC-PAC / NorPAC / UDP:** No confirmed 2026 gov primary endorsement for either Rose or Blackburn.
- **Tennessee establishment:** Leading in early GOP county-level straw polls per his campaign; county-party chairs more split between Rose and Blackburn than polling suggests.
- **Freedom Caucus:** Not a publicly confirmed HFC member as of the 119th Congress.

### Snitched signal summary — Rose
- **Israel-aid stance:** Maximalist, Christian-Zionist framing. Voted YES on every Israel supplemental; explicit NO on Ukraine. "Only Democratic ally" rhetoric.
- **AIPAC ties:** Moderate — not a TrackAIPAC top spotlight (Blackburn occupies that slot) and no confirmed CUFI/RJC speakerships, but his votes are fully aligned with AIPAC's priorities.
- **Foreign-aid posture:** America-First-with-Israel-exception. The classic GOP CUFI-hawk pattern. NOT Fritts-style blanket isolationism.
- **Biggest red flags:**
  1. **2020 election denial + Jan 6 certification objection + Texas v. PA amicus**; voted AGAINST Capitol Police Gold Medal.
  2. **Chelsea Doss age-gap marriage** (met at 17, 24-year gap) — reputational/character vector (The American Prospect 2022).
  3. Unilaterally **blocked $19.1B disaster relief** on the House floor in 2019 — signature contrarian-Freedom-Caucus-style move; unusually harsh constituent-services optics for a gov candidate.
